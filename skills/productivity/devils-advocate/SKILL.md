---
name: devils-advocate
description: Challenges any topic, idea, plan, or position by surfacing the strongest objections, weakest assumptions, and most compelling counterarguments. Use when stress-testing a decision before committing, exposing blind spots in a proposal, or pressure-testing reasoning — or when the user says "play devil's advocate", "devil's advocate this", "argue against this", "poke holes in this", "what's wrong with this", "challenge my thinking".
---

Spawn a subagent to build the strongest possible case *against* the target. Determine the target from the session first; if it is unclear, ask what to challenge.

The reflex to be balanced is the enemy. Never instruct the subagent to be fair, weigh both sides, or concede what works — its only job is to find what is wrong, weak, or risky. This is not steelmanning the opposition: a steelman finds the best version of an argument; a devil's advocate finds the strongest reasons it fails. Go after the assumptions the position cannot survive losing, the objections a sharp critic would raise, and the second-order effects that undermine it. Carry only the sharpest material — mild, easily answered objections dilute the challenge.

Return the challenge directly: no preamble, no softening, no "to be fair." A few sharp paragraphs, more only if the topic demands it.
